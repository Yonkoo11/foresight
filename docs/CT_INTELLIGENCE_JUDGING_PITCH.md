# CT Intelligence Redesign — Hackathon Judging Pitch

> How the Intelligence page redesign strengthens Foresight's demo for judges

---

## Judges Will Ask This Question

**"Show me how a new player learns to draft."**

Currently, the answer is weak:
1. New player lands on Draft page
2. Sees 100 unknown CT influencers
3. No context, no guidance
4. Has to open Twitter to research manually
5. **Problem:** Demo fails, judges see "this needs 5 more hours of onboarding"

---

## With CT Intelligence Redesign, Answer is Compelling

**"New player path to first draft:"**
1. Land on home → "Try drafting"
2. Go to Draft page → See "Need research?" link → Opens Intel
3. Intel page shows **"This Week's Trending"** with actual weekly breakdowns
   - "S-Tier Top 3: @Hsaka (87 pts), @dcfcarpenter (72 pts), @bneilson (65 pts)"
   - Breakdown visible: "87 = 28 activity + 45 engagement + 12 growth + 2 viral"
   - Judges see: "Oh, that's transparent. I understand how they score."
4. New player sees **"Scouted Players Trending"** (personalized)
   - "You looked at 3 players. 2 are heating up this week."
   - Creates: "I should draft them!" momentum
5. New player picks a team in <5 minutes
6. Clicks "Contest Preview" → sees leaderboard → sees their team competing
7. **Judge sees:** "From signup to leaderboard in 90 seconds. They did the hard part right."

## Talking Points for Judges (If They Ask)

**"Your Intel page looks simple. Why should we care?"**

Answer (25 seconds):
> "Most fantasy apps give you league standings. We give you *research tools*. See the weekly score breakdown? That teaches players how scoring works in the first 30 seconds. Consistency metric? That's from DraftKings — lets you know your floor and ceiling. We borrowed the best UX from the best platforms and made it transparent. Because if players understand the game, they stay."

**"How is this different from Twitter?"**

Answer (20 seconds):
> "Twitter is chaos. Intel is structure. You see tiers (S/A/B/C), you see weekly performance broken down (activity, engagement, growth, viral), you see momentum (is this person heating up or cooling down). And it's all your personalized intelligence: your team's score this week, your watchlist players who are trending, what the meta is."

**"Why weekly scores instead of daily?"**

Answer (15 seconds):
> "Daily is noise. Weekly is signal. Influencers vary day-to-day, but a weekly score tells you their real value. It also matches our contest cycle: Monday-Sunday, 4x daily updates to a weekly total. Intelligence should answer: will this person help me win THIS week?"
