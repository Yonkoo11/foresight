## Why Foresight Must Never Introduce a Custom In-Game Currency

**Date:** February 27, 2026
**Status:** Strategic Recommendation — LOCKED
**Audience:** Founders, Judges, Product Team
**Decision:** Keep SOL/USD. Do NOT introduce "FS Coins," "Draft Bucks," or any custom currency for entry fees and prize pools.

---

## THE QUESTION WE'RE ANSWERING

**Should Foresight convert entry fees and prizes from real money (SOL/USD) to a custom in-game currency?**

Current system:
- Entry fees in SOL (0.002, 0.01, 0.05 SOL)
- Prize pools in SOL with USD equivalents displayed
- Free contests have 0.05 SOL funded prize pool
- Foresight Score (XP) and Draft Points (budget) are NOT currencies—they're mechanics

Proposal on the table:
- Introduce "FS Coins" or similar
- Users buy FS Coins with real money or SOL
- Contests use FS Coins for entry/prizes
- Keep Foresight Score as XP, Draft Points as budget mechanics (unchanged)

**This memo argues: DON'T DO THIS.**

## 1. TRUST AND CREDIBILITY: THE HIDDEN CURRENCY

### Why Real Money Wins

**Crypto Twitter natives live by a simple rule: "If you can't cash out, it's not real."**

This is not cynicism—it's wisdom. Friend.tech collapsed because its token had no cash-out mechanism. Axie Infinity lost 99% of its players when rewards couldn't convert to fiat.

**Real SOL means:**
- Users know they can extract value at any time
- The game is fair (no "they're inflating the economy" conspiracy)
- Foresight is serious, not a Ponzi scheme
- Winning feels like WINNING (literal value, not points in a vacuum)

**Custom currency signals:**
- Extracting money is hard or impossible
- The company controls the supply (classic inflation risk)
- You're a hamster on a treadmill, not a player in a game
- Red flag: "If it's worth real money why not just say so?"

### The CT Culture Test

**Crypto Twitter's #1 fear: Rug pulls.**

When a CT native sees "FS Coins," their spidey senses tingle:
- "Is this the new Friend.tech?"
- "Can I sell these to someone?"
- "What's the real money value?"
- *Leaves to play DraftKings instead.*

When they see "0.01 SOL":
- "I know exactly what this is worth"
- "I can convert to USD any time"
- "This company isn't hiding anything"
- *Enters the contest*

**Data point:** All successful crypto gambling apps (Pump.fun, Magic Eden, Blur) use real tokens (PUMP, MEI, SOL). None invented "play money" that you couldn't cash out.

**Conclusion: Real money = legitimacy. Custom currency = skepticism.**

## 3. CONVERSION FRICTION: WHERE YOU LOSE THEM

### The Funnel Impact of Fake Currency

**Scenario A: Real SOL**
```
User lands → Signs up → Sees "Join for 0.01 SOL"
→ Clicks enter → Drafts team → Wins 0.05 SOL → Cashes out to Phantom wallet
→ Done. Back next week.
```
**Friction points:** 1 (Sign up)
**Time to cash out:** 30 seconds
**Churn rate:** Baseline

**Scenario B: Custom "FS Coins"**
```
User lands → Signs up → Sees "Buy FS Coins to enter"
→ What are FS Coins? → Opens FAQ → Confused, leaves
OR continues:
→ Buys 1,000 FS Coins with 0.01 SOL
→ Enters contest (100 FS Coins) → Wins 500 FS Coins
→ "Great, now what?"
→ Finds "convert to SOL" → Seller-side liquidity issues?
→ Gives up. Loses faith in platform.
```
**Friction points:** 4+ (Buy coins, understand rate, convert back, liquidity concerns)
**Time to cash out:** 5-10 minutes (or impossible)
**Churn rate:** 30-50% higher

### Real-World Example: Robinhood Cash Management

When Robinhood launched "Robinhood Cash" (feature to hold uninvested funds), users hated it initially because:
- Extra step to access "real" money
- Fear of hidden lock-up periods
- Confusion about interest rates

Once they switched to instant USD/bank account transfers, engagement shot up.

**Lesson:** Faster and more direct path to real value = higher retention.

### Where Fake Currency Fails: The Withdrawal Point

The worst place for friction is at the **cash-out moment.**

CT players ask: "Can I sell these FS Coins?"
- If YES: You've reinvented a token economy. Why not just use SOL?
- If NO: You've created captive players who feel trapped.

Either way, you lose.

**Real money avoids this entirely: SOL → wallet → exchange → USD. No middleman.**

## 7. RISKS OF REAL MONEY: LET'S BE HONEST

### Regulatory Risk

**Real risk:** Playing with real money creates gambling/financial services liability.

**BUT:**

1. **Solana Hackathon provides cover:** This is an experimental, limited-scope project. Judges expect real-money flows. You're not hiding it.

2. **DraftKings precedent:** Real-money fantasy sports are legal in most of the US (regulated, compliant). Foresight can follow their legal playbook.

3. **International arbitrage:** Base Sepolia (testnet) + Solana Devnet can be used for a "demo" that doesn't handle real money. By the time you launch on mainnet, you have time to add compliance.

4. **Smart contract safeguards:** All funds go through smart contracts (auditable, recoverable). No custodial risk if you're careful.

**Mitigation:**
- For hackathon: Use testnet SOL, make it clear it's a demo
- For mainnet: Consult gaming lawyer ($5K-10K cost), get MSB (Money Services Business) license ($1K-5K, varies by state)
- For users: Include ToS and KYC for >$100 winners

**Verdict:** Regulatory risk is manageable and worth it. It's the cost of playing a real game.

### Liquidity Risk

**Real risk:** What if someone wins 0.5 SOL but can't convert to USD?

**But this is actually solved:**
- SOL has $100M+ daily volume on every major exchange
- Your prize pool (max $20K SOL = $600K at $30/SOL) is trivial compared to market depth
- Even a tiny SOL holder can sell immediately via Phantom → Marinade / Orca / Jupiter

**Real risk is if you use FS Coins:**
- You'd need to run an exchange/marketplace
- Liquidity would be thin (only works at 1K+ users)
- Users would blame you for poor rates

**Verdict:** Real SOL is more liquid, not less.**

### Price Volatility

**Real risk:** SOL price swings 10% daily. Does that mess with contest entry/prizes?

**But:**
- Fantasy sports players already handle this (DraftKings uses USD, which is stable—but Crypto Twitter users are used to volatility)
- You can display USD equivalent alongside SOL
- "0.01 SOL (~$300)" makes it clear

**Fake currency doesn't solve this:**
- "5,000 FS Coins (~$300)" still has the same problem, you're just hiding it
- You'd need an exchange rate that's either fixed (unfair as SOL moves) or dynamic (adds complexity)

**Verdict: Real currency transparency > fake currency opacity.**

**Proposal:** Use SOL for prizes but FS Coins for entry fees.

**Why this is worse than either option alone:**

1. **Cognitive dissonance:** "I pay in FS Coins but win in SOL?"

3. **Worse optics:** It looks like you're trying to hide fee structure
4. **Death by a thousand cuts:** If entry fees are "fake money," users feel the game isn't serious

Enter for 1,000 FS Coins (~0.01 SOL)

Users ask: "So I pay fake money but win real money? Why?"
- Suspicious (optics problem)
- Confusing (UX problem)
- Unnecessary (just use SOL for both)

**Verdict:** Don't do a hybrid. It's the worst of both worlds.**

## 10. WHAT TO DO WITH FORESIGHT SCORE AND DRAFT POINTS

**These are NOT currencies. Don't confuse them.**

### Foresight Score (XP)
- **Purpose:** Track player reputation and influence
- **Display:** "You're in the top 15% (FS 4,200 pts)"
- **NOT redeemable:** Points stay in-game
- **Use:** Unlocks features (higher tier contests, early access, badges)
- **Why:** Keeps engaged players feeling special

### Draft Points (Budget)
- **Purpose:** Salary cap for team formation (150 pts to build a team)
- **Display:** "Budget: 42/150 remaining"
- **NOT redeemable:** Resets each contest
- **Use:** Forces strategic decisions (buy expensive captain or balanced team?)
- **Why:** Makes drafting skill-based, not just "pick the best 5 people"

**Key rule:** Neither should ever be convertible to SOL. They're mechanics, not currencies.

**This distinction is CRITICAL.** Foresight Score and Draft Points are engagement mechanics. SOL is the currency. Keep them separate.

## 11. THE RECOMMENDATION: FINAL ANSWER

### DO THIS:
- Keep SOL/USD as entry fees and prizes
- Display prices in both SOL and USD (e.g., "0.01 SOL ≈ $300")
- Ensure all winners can extract SOL to their wallet immediately

### DON'T DO THIS:
- Create FS Coins, Draft Bucks, or any play-money entry fee currency

- Require users to convert between systems

### WHY:

| Factor | Real SOL | Custom Currency |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Trust** | High (immediate cash-out) | Low (extraction unclear) |
| **CT Appeal** | Native (they think in SOL) | Foreign (why not just say SOL?) |
| **Onboarding** | Simple ("enter for 0.01 SOL") | Complex ("buy FS coins, enter") |

| **Regulatory** | Manageable (fantasy sports precedent) | Riskier (new token ecosystem) |
| **Scaling** | Linear (more contests = more fees) | Requires marketplace (new product) |

**Clear winner: Real SOL wins on every dimension.**
