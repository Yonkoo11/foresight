People share what:
1. Makes them look smart ✅ (High credibility score = proof of insight)

3. Creates status ✅ (First 1K founding members get permanent badge)
4. Lets them compete ✅ (Climbing rankings is addictive)


## 11. ANTI-PLAYBOOK: What NOT to Do

### Marketing Mistakes to Avoid

#### **❌ 1. The Token Trap**

**What not to do:**
- Promise token airdrop in Week 1
- Show detailed token economics too early
- Use "$FORESIGHT" branding before it exists
- Say "XP converts to tokens" when it doesn't

**Why it fails:**
- Creates false expectations
- Burns credibility when token gets delayed
- Attracts farmers, not real users
- Looks like ponzi (kills influencer partnerships)

**What to do instead:**
- Stay vague: "Early supporters will be rewarded"
- Show XP tracking (foundation for future)
- Wait until Month 3-4 to talk token
- Never give dates or numbers

#### **❌ 2. The Influencer Overpromise**

**What not to do:**
- Offer big money upfront (you don't have it)
- Promise "lifetime 5% rake" without thinking through math
- Make deals with Tier 1 influencers before testing with Tier 2
- Ask for exclusivity

**Why it fails:**
- You can't afford it
- They'll disappear when you can't pay
- Limits your ability to partner with others
- Creates legal liability

**What to do instead:**
- Start with no payment (test commitment)
- Only pay when you have revenue (Month 2+)
- Start with Tier 2-3 (cheaper, more grateful)
- Always do rev-share (aligns incentives)

#### **❌ 3. The Engagement Bait**

**What not to do:**
- "Retweet to win 0.5 ETH!"
- "Follow + comment for airdrop"
- Tag 5 friends and you get points
- Fake urgency ("Last 24 hours!")

**Why it fails:**
- Attracts bot engagement
- Kills organic reach (Twitter algo hates it)
- False signups don't convert to DAU
- Looks desperate

**What to do instead:**
- Let quality content drive engagement
- Create real FOMO (founding members closing)
- Make sharing valuable (shareable cards, proof)
- Play the long game (consistency matters)

#### **❌ 4. The Premature Monetization**

**What not to do:**
- Launch paid contests in Week 1
- Ask for USD/crypto in beta
- Gate features behind paywall early
- Use "Season Pass" to get early $

**Why it fails:**
- Kills viral coefficient (people don't invite friends to paid games)
- Raises expectations (people want perfection if they pay)
- Creates churn (people try once, don't return)
- Shows you're desperate for money

**What to do instead:**
- Free everything Month 1
- Sponsorship/partnerships for early revenue
- Launch paid in Month 2-3 (when DAU is strong)
- Make free tier valuable enough to keep people

#### **❌ 5. The Unfocused Marketing**

**What not to do:**
- Post on 10 different platforms daily
- Try to grow Reddit while ignoring Twitter
- Answer every comment and DM
- Launch 5 initiatives simultaneously

**Why it fails:**
- You have 1 engineer, limited time
- Spread too thin = mediocre execution everywhere
- Influencers can tell you're not serious
- Burn out quickly

**What to do instead:**
- Focus: Twitter + Referral (Month 1)
- Add Discord when you have influencers (Month 1.5)
- Test Farcaster (Month 2)
- Ignore everything else (Reddit, TikTok, LinkedIn)

#### **❌ 6. The Spam Outreach**

**What not to do:**
- DM 100 random influencers the same message
- Try to close deals in first DM
- DM every hour until they respond
- Pitch multiple times to someone who said no

**Why it fails:**
- Looks like bot
- Gets muted/blocked
- Creates negative reputation
- Wastes time on non-responders

**What to do instead:**
- DM top 20 influencers personally
- Personalized messages (reference their work)
- One ask per conversation
- Accept "no" gracefully
- Follow up only once, 7 days later

#### **❌ 7. The Feature Creep**

**What not to do:**
- Add 10 features in Week 1
- Change UI constantly
- Add new leaderboard types every day
- Keep old features when launching new ones

**Why it fails:**
- Confuses users (what should I do?)
- Creates bugs (more code = more breaks)
- Dilutes your focus
- Makes roadmap meaningless

**What to do instead:**
- Launch 1 feature at a time
- Test it for 1 week minimum
- Only remove old features when replacing
- Communicate changes clearly
