## 1. What Does "Influence" Actually Mean in CT Right Now (Early 2026)?

### The Evolution: What Changed Since 2020-2021

**2020-2021 Bull Run:**
- Influence = follower count + viral tweet frequency
- Callout accuracy secondary (everyone was up, "broken clock" effect)
- Network effects minimal (communities siloed: defi-native, NFT-native, trading-native)
- DeFi narratives dominated (@aantonop, @SBF_FTX, @ElonMusk's occasional tweet)

**2022-2024 Winter:**
- Follower count became *unreliable* (people left, bot armies exposed)
- Callout accuracy became critical (who predicted Luna collapse? Who saw FTX coming?)
- Network density exploded (Farcaster, Discord communities, telegram groups)
- Building (shipping products) became the new signal
- Alpha leaks and early access gained more weight

**2026 Reality (Now):**
- Influence = **trustworthiness × callout accuracy × community density**
- Follower count is table stakes (need 5K+ to be heard), not the differentiator
- Specialization matters (you're "a Bitcoin person," "NFT expert," "trading guru")
- Skin in the game is everything (do they actually hold what they promote?)
- Real-time Twitter volume is declining; Farcaster + Discord + private TG groups = real influence

### The Four Pillars of CT Influence (What We Actually Respect)

**1. Callout Accuracy (40% of total influence weight in CT culture)**
- Did they predict market moves? (Correctly called the $60K bottom in 2022, etc.)
- Do they have a track record of being right on protocol launches, governance changes, token economics?
- Did they warn about FTX/Luna/3AC before the collapse?
- This is the hardest signal to quantify in a scoring system, but the most respected

**2. Alpha Quality (25%)**
- Do they share information before it becomes common knowledge?
- Do they have real relationships with founders, VCs, protocol teams?
- Do their newsletter/Discord posts give actionable intelligence?
- Example: First to spot a new Uniswap V4 alpha = 10x respect vs. tweeting Uniswap price observations

**3. Skin in the Game (20%)**
- Do they actually hold the tokens they recommend?
- Do they move portfolio along with their calls?
- Are they transparent about conflicts? (e.g., "I'm a UNI DAO voter" vs. hiding it)
- CT users despise hidden incentives more than anything else

**4. Community Density (15%)**
- Do people *rally around* this person's calls?
- Do their posts get quoted 1000x in group chats?
- Do they have a Discord/Farcaster community that acts on their insights?
- Following is passive; community density is active

### What Separates Real CT Influencers from Fakes (The Authenticity Test)

**Real Influencers (The Ones Who Matter):**
- Interact with their community (DMs, Spaces, Discord)
- Make wrong calls AND admit them publicly
- Share losses, not just wins
- Have a clearly stated thesis (not just "hype everything")
- Unique takes on narratives (not just repeating what @APompliano said)
- Multi-year track record (can trace their Twitter history)

**Fake/Bot-Inflated Influencers (The Ones Who Fade):**
- 100K followers, but tweets get 50 likes (bot followers)
- Only tweet bullish (never admit being wrong)
- Every tweet mentions a token (obvious shilling)
- Recent account with sudden follower spike (purchased or airdropped bots)
- No personality, just retweets popular takes
- Disappear during downturns (real people stay, fakes dump and ghost)

**The "Whale With a Twitter" Phenomenon:**
- Someone dumps 50 ETH and suddenly has influence
- Followers think they can front-run their buys
- Influence evaporates if they sell or go quiet for 2 weeks
- CT recognizes this as ephemeral (they joke: "another 10-day influence cycle")

## 2. What Makes a Week "Good" vs "Bad" for a CT Influencer?

### The Virality Patterns (What Drives CT Engagement Spikes)

**A Good Week for an Influencer (Probability of High Activity Spike):**

1. **Market Volatility (Price Moves >5% in 24h)**
   - Bitcoin/ETH moves → all eyes on market analysts
   - People want to know: "Is this a bounce or a rug?"
   - Influencers who called the direction get amplified
   - Engagement typically 3-5x normal baseline

2. **Protocol Governance Drama**
   - Uniswap DAO votes on fee tiers
   - MakerDAO debates collateral changes
   - Aave governance proposal on discount loans
   - Insiders and governance nerds get massive engagement
   - Example: @0xKarl, @LarksDAO can get 10K+ engagements on governance posts others would get 100 on

3. **Token Launches / Ecosystem Announcements**
   - New layer 2 mainnet deployment
   - Stablecoin launch
   - Major integration announcement (e.g., "Ethereum gets Account Abstraction EIP finalized")
   - First-to-tweet advantage is real here (10x engagement if you're first with good analysis)

4. **Controversy / Drama**
   - VC funding drama (e.g., "Sequoia backs AI token")
   - Founder callouts ("This founder is running exit scam")
   - Regulatory news (SEC enforcement actions)
   - Faked/incorrect drama (someone says $X is a scam, chaos ensues, correction follows)
   - CT feeds on drama like traditional media feeds on celebrity gossip

5. **Memes and Cultural Moments**
   - New meme format emerges (e.g., 2021's "when Ethereum" memes)
   - April Fools jokes that fool people
   - Celebrity/influencer drama that bleeds into crypto ("Elon just said...")
   - Community in-jokes (e.g., "Have fun staying poor" responses)
   - Engagement can be 2-3x for the right meme at the right time

6. **Influencer Callout or Validation**
   - @VitalikButerin retweets your analysis
   - @SBF_FTX responds to your criticism (before collapse, this was huge)
   - Multiple popular accounts quote your take
   - Being in the retweet chain of someone with 500K followers = 5-10x engagement spike

**A Bad Week for an Influencer:**

- Market flatlines (no urgency, low engagement baseline)
- They go silent (vacation, focus on building, personal issues)
- Their recent prediction proved wrong (people mute/unfollow)
- Larger story eclipses the crypto market (US election drama, war, etc.)
- They tweet only about personal life (no alpha, no analysis)

### Virality Predictability: The CT Native's Assessment

**Question: Is virality predictable for fantasy league scoring?**

**Answer: 60% predictable, 40% random.**

**The Predictable 60% (Can Be Scored):**
- Market volatility is forecastable (use volatility index, options data)
- Token launches are scheduled
- Governance votes are calendared
- Regulatory decisions have patterns
- If an influencer has a 30% historical "callout accuracy," they'll likely get 3-5x engagement during volatile weeks

**The Chaotic 40% (Pure Randomness):**
- A random meme goes viral unpredictably
- A single tweet gets ratio'd (attacked) unexpectedly
- Elon tweets something, chaos follows
- An influencer's personal story goes viral (health issue, huge trade loss, etc.)
- A bot army amplifies someone's tweet artificially

**Scoring Implication:** You can predict activity (tweets-per-volatile-market-day) but not engagement reliably. The formula in fantasyScoringService.ts uses engagement rate, which is smart—it normalizes for unpredictable spikes.

## 3. What Scoring Signals Would CT Users RESPECT?

### The BS Meter: What Would Get Called Out Immediately

**CT Power Users Would Reject These Metrics:**
- Pure follower count ("That's easily fakeable")
- Tweet volume alone ("Spam tweets aren't influence")
- Simple engagement rate ("Bot engagement doesn't count")
- Retweet volume ("Proportional to follower count, not quality")
- Trending hashtag mentions ("Who cares if you trend for 2 hours?")

**CT Power Users Would ACCEPT These Metrics:**

1. **Engagement Rate (With Filters)**
   - Yes, if it's weighted by follower count (small accounts' high engagement rates matter more)
   - Example: 5K follower account with 10% engagement > 100K account with 2% engagement
   - Current formula: sqrt(likes + retweets×2 + replies×3) is respect-worthy
   - Why it works: Retweets and replies indicate "people think this is worth amplifying," not just passive likes

2. **Callout Accuracy (HARD to Implement)**
   - This is what CT cares about most
   - But automatable? Almost impossible
   - Could use: "Did this influencer's price predictions happen within 2 weeks?"
   - Could use: "Did they tweet about a token before it launched?"
   - Hard to implement at scale; suggestion: Leave this as "Spotlight bonus" for community-voted top influencers

3. **Follower Growth + Quality (IF Weighted Correctly)**
   - Growth in 1 week is meaningful (people actively following = they respect you)
   - But only count if growth > 100 followers (filter out bot armies)
   - Current formula: "1 pt per 2K followers gained" is fair
   - Why it works: Means real people are deciding to follow mid-contest

4. **Volatility-Adjusted Engagement**
   - On high-volatility days (BTC move > 5%), influencers get more engagement (expected)
   - On flat days (no news, no moves), engagement is baseline
   - Influencers who maintain engagement on "nothing news" days are more skillful
   - Suggestion: Weight engagement by market volatility (high volatility week = engagement 0.8x, flat week = engagement 1.2x)

5. **Retweet-to-Reply Ratio**
   - Retweets = "I agree" (passive)
   - Replies = "I have a point" (active, adds value)
   - High reply ratio indicates community building and discourse
   - Current formula: repliesWeight: 3 vs likesWeight: 1 is smart
   - Why it works: CT values discourse over passive consumption

### The Fairness Test: What Advantages/Disadvantages Different Account Types?

**Early Concern: Engagement Rate Favors Small Accounts**

Scenario:
- Account A: 10K followers, 2,000 engagements (20% engagement rate)
- Account B: 500K followers, 30,000 engagements (6% engagement rate)

Current formula (weighted): Account A would score higher on engagement despite having 1/50th the reach.

**CT Reaction:** "This is actually fair. The small account proved something interesting. Engagement rate is THE signal."

**Counter-concern: Volume Bias**
- An account tweeting 50 times per day (spam) vs. 3 per day (quality)
- Current formula: "min(35, tweets_this_week × 1.5)" caps activity at 23 tweets/week
- Means 24+ tweets/week = same score as 23 tweets/week (perfect)

**CT Reaction:** "Good. You're preventing tweet-spam rewards."

**Counter-concern: Viral Luck**
- Someone tweets at perfect time, goes viral, gets 100K engagements that week
- Next week, nothing
- Current formula: Averages across tweetsAnalyzed (normalizes for inconsistency)

**CT Reaction:** "Fair. One viral week doesn't make you influential forever."

## 6. Red Flags: What CT Power Users Would Immediately Call Out (Gaming the System)

### How the Scoring System Could Be Gamed (And Why Each Would Be Obvious)

**Gaming Attempt #1: Buying Followers**

- Create fake follower army (500K → 600K followers, all bots)
- Growth score jumps artificially
- **Why it fails:** Engagement rate collapses (6% → 0.5% fake engagement)
- **CT Reaction:** Immediate callout ("Their followers are 80% bots")
- **How to prevent:** Weight engagement rate above raw follower count (already done)
- **Better metric:** Look for "unusual growth pattern" (gain 100K followers in 1 week = red flag)

**Gaming Attempt #2: Tweet Spam During Volatile Markets**

- Influencer tweets 50 times/day when BTC moves 5%
- Activity score maxes out at 35, so no additional benefit
- But engagement could accumulate (more tweets = more chance to get retweets)
- **Why it fails:** replieWeight and retweet patterns don't change; quantity doesn't drive quality engagement
- **CT Reaction:** "They're spamming. Muting them."
- **How to prevent:** Current cap at 35 is sufficient. Could also: penalize >5 tweets/hour

**Gaming Attempt #3: Engagement Ring / Bot Amplification**

- Pay engagement ring (group that likes/retweets each other's posts)
- Artificially boost engagement metrics
- **Why it fails:** Engagement becomes hollow (high metrics, low follower growth, no new followers)
- **CT Reaction:** Immediate detection ("This is an engagement ring")
- **How to prevent:** Weight new follower count + check for ratio (engagement:followers should stay 1-3%)

**Gaming Attempt #4: "Going Viral" Artificially**

- Partner with bot network to make one tweet seem viral (1M impressions, 10K engagements)
- Viral score spikes
- **Why it fails:** Requires consistency. One viral tweet doesn't make a career; next week is baseline
- **CT Reaction:** "That tweet was weird, did they buy engagement?"
- **How to prevent:** Look for pattern detection (if viral metrics are 10x above baseline, flag it)

**Gaming Attempt #5: Token Shilling**

- Partner with project: "Tweet our token, we'll reward you"
- Engagement spikes because community is watching/trading
- **Why it fails:** Follower growth doesn't match engagement (they're trading, not following)
- **CT Reaction:** "They're getting paid to shill. Their opinion is worthless."
- **How to prevent:** This is cultural (can't be automated). Recommendation: Flag accounts with obvious shilling pattern (tweets only tokens from specific VC)

**Gaming Attempt #6: Sleeper Account Manipulation**

- Influencer creates 10 accounts, coordinates them to all mention each other
- Looks like a community rallying, but it's one person
- **Why it fails:** Follower ratios would be weird (same 100 people follow all accounts)
- **CT Reaction:** Easy to detect (community recognizes coordinated accounts)
- **How to prevent:** Can't prevent at platform level, but users will call it out. Recommendation: Have moderation flags

### What's Actually Fair Game (Legal Gaming)

**Things That Are Legit Strategies (Not Cheating):**

1. **Timing tweets for optimal engagement**
   - Tweet at 6 AM UTC when US is waking up
   - Tweet during market volatility when engagement is highest
   - **Not cheating because:** It's skill (knowing your audience)

2. **Building a community (organic engagement ring)**
   - Create a Discord that genuinely engages with your content
   - They amplify your tweets because they like you
   - **Not cheating because:** Real relationship building

3. **Going deep on a narrative**
   - Tweet 3-4 related threads on one topic that explodes
   - Get 10K engagements in one day
   - **Not cheating because:** Skill at narrative timing

4. **Getting co-signed by bigger accounts**
   - Do good analysis, get retweeted by @VitalikButerin
   - Engagement spikes
   - **Not cheating because:** Earned credibility

## 7. The Scoring System Assessment: Does It Respect CT Culture?

### Foresight's Current Scoring Formula (From fantasyScoringService.ts)

```
Weekly Score = Activity (0-35) + Engagement (0-60) + Growth (0-40) + Viral (0-25)

ACTIVITY: 1.5 pts per tweet, cap 35 (prevents spam)
ENGAGEMENT: sqrt(weighted_engagement) × 1.5, capped 60 (rewards quality over quantity)
GROWTH: 1 pt per 2K followers + 5 pts per 1% growth, cap 40 (mixed absolute + relative)
VIRAL: 4-12 pts per ultra-high-engagement tweet, cap 25 (rewards moments)
```

### CT Native's Assessment: 8/10 (Very Respectful)

**What Works (Why CT Would Accept This):**

✅ **Activity Cap Prevents Spam**
- 23 tweets/week maximum benefit = rewards consistent daily presence, not ADHD tweeting
- CT respects thoughtfulness; rewards consistency

✅ **Engagement Weighted by Interaction Type**
- Replies (3x) > Retweets (2x) > Likes (1x)
- This is exactly right: replies show discourse, retweets show agreement, likes show passive interest
- CT culture values discourse > passive consumption

✅ **Growth Component Normalizes Account Size**
- Small accounts getting 500 new followers = 0.25 pts
- Large accounts getting 50K new followers = 25 pts
- Respects both (small accounts' growth is rarer, so it counts)
- But doesn't penalize large accounts unfairly

✅ **Captain Bonus (1.5x, Not 2x)**
- Punishes you less for bad captain picks
- Encourages strategic captain deployment
- 1.5x feels right for CT (enough to matter, not game-breaking)

✅ **Spotlight Bonus (12/8/4 Flat Points)**
- Top voted influencers get recognition
- Flat points (not %) means smaller accounts can still win with these bonuses
- Encourages voting engagement

**What Could Be Better (Constructive Feedback):**

⚠️ **Viral Score Estimation Is Weak**
- Current method: Estimate based on "if average engagement is very high"
- Problem: Can't distinguish between:
  - Real viral tweet (100K engagements, 1 tweet, 10K followers)
  - Consistent high-engagement baseline (10K followers, 500 engagements per tweet)
- Suggestion: If individual tweet data were available, use threshold detection (tweets >2 SD above account's normal engagement)
- Impact: Medium (affects only ultra-viral weeks, which are rare)

⚠️ **Growth Rate Might Reward Bought Followers**
- Current: "1 pt per 2K followers gained" + "5 pts per 1% growth"
- Problem: Someone gaining 10K followers in 1 day (all bots) would spike hard
- Suggestion: Add flag if growth is >2 SD above normal (80%+ growth spikes are sus)
- Impact: Low (can be detected and flagged post-hoc; doesn't break the system)

⚠️ **No Callout Accuracy Signal**
- Current system measures activity + engagement + growth, but not *correctness*
- Problem: Someone could get high engagement by being wrong (controversy)
- Suggestion: This is hard to automate. Keep as is, or add optional "Community Callout Accuracy" vote
- Impact: Medium (this is what CT cares about most, but can't be automated easily)

⚠️ **Viral Detection Might Undercount Rising Stars**
- Someone going from 100 engagements/tweet to 500 engagements/tweet (5x growth)
- In absolute terms, only 400 more engagements, but relative it's huge
- Current system would count this as engagement growth, which is fair
- Suggestion: None—system handles this well with relative weighting
