## THE BOOTSTRAP PATH (Recommended if Possible)

### Can You Actually Bootstrap?

**Reality check:**

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| **Monthly burn** | $500-750 | Low - infrastructure only |
| **Founder runway** | 24+ months | Can you work for free for 2 years? |
| **Revenue by M9** | $1,500+/month needed | Aggressive but achievable |
| **Willpower** | ??? | Solo work is psychologically hard |

**If you can stomach 18-24 months of zero income: Bootstrap is best.**

### Bootstrap Milestones

```
Months 1-3:  Build product, get 500 users, measure retention
Months 4-6:  Organic growth, referral program, verify economics
Months 7-9:  Hit breakeven ($1,500/month revenue = $500 cost)
Months 10-12: Scale to profitability ($3,000+/month revenue)
Months 13-18: Optimize for exit or Series A
Months 19-24: Exit/Series A conversation

If you hit this timeline: You never need external funding.
You own 100% of the company worth ~$8M-15M in 2027.
```

**Advantages of bootstrap:**
- Keep 100% equity (no dilution)
- Forced discipline on costs
- Only answer to yourself
- Entire upside is yours
- Can iterate without VC pressure

**Disadvantages:**
- Slow growth (organic only, no paid ads)
- Lonely (no co-founders, no team)
- High personal burn (no salary)
- Vulnerable to competition (better-funded competitor enters)


**Term sheet basics:**
- **Type:** SAFE or simple equity investment (not convertible note - too expensive)

**Why these terms?**
- SAFE is faster/cheaper than equity agreement
- 20% discount aligns investor interests
- Non-participating preference keeps upside intact for you
- No board seat keeps you in control


### During Meeting

**Structure:**
1. **Story (3 min):** Problem → insight → solution
2. **Product Demo (5 min):** Show it working, actual users if possible
3. **Traction (2 min):** Metrics, growth trajectory
4. **Ask (1 min):** "We're raising $300K to hit 5K users by Month 12"
5. **Questions (9 min):** Let them drive conversation

**Responses to Common Questions:**

> "How do you compete with DraftKings?"
- "DraftKings is traditional fantasy. We're focused on crypto native + prediction markets. They have $4B players, we have 3M crypto Twitter users. Different markets."

> "What if Pump.fun or Blur launches this?"
- "They could. But we're already 6 months ahead on data. We have 18 months of influencer scoring. Even if they copy tomorrow, we'll have 2 years of data by the time they launch. Plus, our users are rooting for us because we're crypto Twitter natives, not centralized platforms."

> "How do you get users with no marketing budget?"
- "Organic. Our users are CT people. When they win, they post about it. Word-of-mouth is free. Referral program incentivizes sharing. By Month 6, we'll have data showing 30%+ of new users come from referrals."

> "What's your TAM?"
- "Immediate: 50K-100K crypto native gamers (TAM: $5M-10M/year). Medium term: 500K+ crypto enthusiasts (TAM: $50M-100M/year). Long term: 10M+ people worldwide (TAM: $1B+). We're starting with the most engaged slice."

> "Why should I invest in you vs [competitor]?"
- "Speed and focus. We're shipping in 6 weeks, not 6 months. We're focused on one thing (influencer prediction), not trying to be everything. And we're founder-led - the product is built by the person who understands crypto Twitter, not a corporate committee."

## DEAL STRUCTURE: Terms & Documents

### Simple SAFE Agreement

**Why SAFE vs equity?**
- Faster (1 page vs 10 pages)
- Cheaper (lawyers $2K vs $5K)
- Founder-friendly (you keep control)
- Investor-friendly (converts to equity later)

**SAFE template from Y Combinator:**
```
Investor: [Name] ($300K)
Valuation cap: $2M
Discount: 20%
MFN + Pro-rata: Yes
Liquidation preference: Non-participating 1x

On Series A (whenever that is), the SAFE converts:
- If Series A at $10M valuation: 20% discount = investor gets 20% more shares
- If Series A at $5M valuation: 20% discount still applies

Simple. Clean. Works for both sides.
```

### Documents You'll Need

```
Legal docs ($5K total from startup lawyer):
[ ] SAFE agreement (boilerplate from Y Combinator)
[ ] Company operating agreement (if not already done)
[ ] Cap table (Excel, updated monthly)
[ ] Term sheet (one page summary)

Investor relations:
[ ] Monthly updates email (5 investors, 5 min to write)
[ ] Quarterly full update (deck + financial update)
[ ] Annual investor meeting (if enough money to justify)
```

## SERIES A: When and How

### Only Raise Series A If

```
✅ DO raise Series A if:
├─ 5K+ MAU and growing 20%+ month-over-month
├─ $50K+/month revenue run rate
├─ Clear path to $1M+ ARR within 12 months
├─ Have competitive pressure (need to scale faster)
└─ Want to own market (not just be profitable)

❌ DON'T raise Series A if:
├─ Just trying to extend runway (bad reason)
├─ No clear path to profitability
├─ Founder burnt out (need to step back, not forward)
├─ Market showing weakness (user acquisition costs rising)
└─ Already profitable and happy (why dilute?)
```

### Series A Numbers

**If you raise Series A:**
- Size: $1M-3M (depends on traction)
- Valuation: 3-5x Series A revenue run rate
  - Example: $50K/month = $600K annual → $1.8M-3M valuation
- Dilution: 25-35% (new investors get that much equity)
- Timeline: Month 12-15 (after seed success)

**Post-Series A cap table example:**

```
Before Series A (after seed):
├─ Founder: 80%
├─ Seed investors: 20%
└─ Total: 100%

After Series A ($2M at $4M pre, new investors get 33%):
├─ Founder: 53% (80% × 2/3)
├─ Seed investors: 13% (20% × 2/3)
├─ Series A investors: 33%
└─ Total: 100%

Founder still owns majority. Good.
```

### Series A Pitch (Different from Seed)

Seed pitch was: "Here's a working product, help us find product-market fit"

Series A pitch is: "We've found product-market fit, help us dominate the market"

```
Series A deck (12 slides):
```

## THE VERDICT: Bootstrap or Raise?

### Decision Framework

**Bootstrap if:**
- You have 18-24 months of personal runway (can work for free)
- You believe in your ability to acquire users organically
- You don't need to be first to market (comfortable moving fast but not insanely)
- You prefer full control and equity ownership
- You're okay being solo for 6-12 months

**Seed Raise if:**
- You have traction by Month 6 (500+ MAU) but want to accelerate
- You need a team to build faster (engineer, marketer)
- You want to move aggressively against competition
- You're okay with dilution for faster growth
- You believe in venture returns (not just bootstrap profits)

### Final Recommendation

**Try to bootstrap. If it's not working by Month 6, raise seed.**

Reasoning:
1. Your costs are low ($500/month) - you can stay independent a long time
2. Your unit economics are good - no need for capital to be viable
3. You'll build discipline and focus on what matters (users, not spending)
4. If you do need funding, you'll have real traction to show (better valuation)
5. If you don't need funding, you'll own 100% of a profitable business

The founder's personal preferences matter more than the business model. Some people thrive as solopreneurs. Others need a team. Know yourself.
